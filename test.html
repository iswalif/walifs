Smartphone Usage and Sleep Analytics

Bangladesh Survey Dataset (N > 1000) — This reference document explains 25 analytical charts derived from survey data on smartphone habits, sleep quality, daytime focus, and well-being. Each chart includes axis descriptions, interpretation guidance, and the hypothesis it tests. The goal is to make data-driven decisions for public health impact. Hypotheses Tested (H1–H25)

  1. H1: High phone use (8 or more hours per day) is linked to poor sleep quality.
  2. H2: Using the phone for 4 or more hours after 10 PM pushes bedtime past midnight.
  3. H3: Poor sleep quality is linked to low daytime feeling scores (1 to 2).
  4. H4: Older teenagers (16 to 18) report worse focus than adults (25 and older) despite similar sleep.
  5. H5: Social media and gaming are dominant activities linked to worse sleep.
  6. H6: There is a negative correlation: more phone hours means fewer sleep hours.
  7. H7: Heavy after-10PM users have lower median focus scores.
  8. H8: A belief–action gap exists: many know phone use hurts sleep but do not reduce use.
  9. H9: Geographic disparities exist in average sleep quality across districts.
  10. H10: The most common pathway is: high daily use, heavy after-10PM use, late bedtime, and poor sleep.
  11. H11: The combination of high phone use, late-night use, and late bedtime creates a profile of low focus and poor sleep.
  12. H12: Good sleepers score better on all well-being dimensions simultaneously.
  13. H13: Sleep quality varies by day of survey submission, suggesting a weekend effect.
  14. H14: Lower daytime feeling scores correlate with significantly higher mean phone hours.
  15. H15: Females report poorer sleep quality than males.
  16. H16: Heavy late users have a sleep distribution shifted toward shorter duration.
  17. H17: Respondents who tried reducing screen time report better sleep.
  18. H18: The combination of social media and gaming after 10 PM is strongly linked to poor sleep.
  19. H19: Shorter sleep duration pushes focus scores toward the low end (1 to 2).
  20. H20: After-10PM use correlates more negatively with sleep quality than total daily use.
  21. H21: Using the phone for 4 or more hours after 10 PM multiplies the odds of poor sleep by approximately 3.5 times, after adjustments.
  22. H22: Delaying bedtime past midnight sharply reduces the chance of good sleep.
  23. H23: Age-specific pathways exist: younger groups show more gaming-related poor sleep.
  24. H24: The high-phone, low-sleep quadrant is densely populated by poor sleepers.
  25. H25: The 4 or more hours after-10PM group has a long tail of very bad daytime feeling scores.
